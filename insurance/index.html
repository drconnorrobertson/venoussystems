Workers' Compensation

A significant portion of our business serves injured workers across the country. Our team understands the nuances of state-by-state workers' comp systems and partners directly with case managers, adjusters, nurse case managers, and treating physicians. We coordinate authorization, deliver equipment, and bill the carrier directly — the injured worker pays nothing.

We work with state workers' compensation systems, federal programs (FECA, Longshore), and self-insured employer plans.

Medicare

Venous Systems is an accredited Medicare DME supplier in good standing. We carry the appropriate accreditations and surety bonds and are bound by Medicare DMEPOS supplier standards. We carefully document medical necessity, manage capped rental schedules, and submit clean claims that meet Medicare's strict documentation requirements — including the specific elements needed for codes such as E0676 (DVT prophylaxis), E0731 (TENS), L0631–L0640 (LSO), L0464 (TLSO), and the various walker codes.

Medicaid

Medicaid coverage varies by state, both in terms of covered HCPCS codes and prior authorization requirements. Our billing specialists are familiar with state Medicaid plans and the larger managed Medicaid organizations across the country. Whenever possible, we secure prior authorization in advance and avoid billing surprises.

Auto / No-Fault & PIP

Personal Injury Protection (PIP) and no-fault auto coverage frequently includes DME for accident-related injuries. We work directly with auto carriers, attorneys, and treating physicians to bill these claims correctly and efficiently — common in motor vehicle accident recovery, whiplash management, and post-MVA orthopedic care.

TRICARE

Venous Systems serves active-duty service members, veterans, and military families through TRICARE coverage. We follow TRICARE's specific authorization and documentation procedures and coordinate with military treatment facilities and civilian network providers.

Commercial Insurance

We are in-network or out-of-network depending on the carrier and the patient's specific plan. Our team verifies benefits before shipping, communicates patient responsibility clearly in advance, and works with the patient on payment plans where appropriate.

How we minimize surprises: Every Venous Systems order goes through pre-shipment benefits verification. If a service is not covered, requires prior authorization that has not yet been obtained, or has unusual patient responsibility, we contact the prescribing provider and the patient before sending the equipment.

What We Need to Submit a Claim

  • A valid prescription with the appropriate diagnosis codes
  • Patient demographic and insurance information
  • Relevant clinical documentation (operative report for post-op equipment, chart notes for chronic conditions)
  • Any prior authorization information already on file
