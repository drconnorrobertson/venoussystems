Post-Op Recovery

Thermal Compression Therapy

Cold therapy and intermittent pneumatic compression in a single device — the most effective non-pharmacologic way to reduce post-surgical swelling and pain.

How Thermal Compression Works

Thermal compression therapy combines two clinically proven recovery modalities into one device. A reservoir circulates chilled water through an anatomically shaped wrap, cooling the surgical site to reduce inflammation and slow nerve conduction. At the same time, the wrap inflates and deflates in programmed cycles, mechanically pumping inflammatory fluid out of the tissue and improving venous return.

The result is the same physiologic effect that surgeons used to rely on bags of ice and elevation to produce — but delivered consistently, comfortably, and through the night when the patient is most likely to be in pain.

Common Indications

  • Total knee arthroplasty (TKA) and total hip arthroplasty (THA)
  • Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) reconstruction and meniscus repair
  • Rotator cuff repair and shoulder arthroscopy
  • Ankle reconstruction and Achilles tendon repair
  • Spinal surgery, including lumbar fusion
  • Soft-tissue trauma and major sprain management

Why Patients and Surgeons Choose Thermal Compression

Clinical benefits documented in peer-reviewed literature:
  • Reduced post-operative pain scores compared to ice alone
  • Lower opioid consumption during the first 7–14 days post-op
  • Less swelling, faster range-of-motion recovery
  • Improved sleep quality during the early recovery window

Insurance & Documentation

Thermal compression units are typically covered under workers' compensation, auto/no-fault, and many commercial plans for post-surgical recovery. Medicare and Medicaid coverage varies by region and indication. Our billing team verifies benefits in advance and prepares the medical necessity documentation, including the surgeon's letter, operative report, and prescription.

Rental and Purchase Options

Most patients receive thermal compression on a short-term rental basis (typically 2–4 weeks following surgery). For chronic conditions or longer recovery windows, purchase is available. We work with the prescribing provider to recommend the most cost-effective and clinically appropriate option.
