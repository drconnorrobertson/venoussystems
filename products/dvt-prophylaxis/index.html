DVT Prophylaxis Compression Devices

Portable, battery-powered intermittent pneumatic compression for patients at elevated risk of deep vein thrombosis — at home, in transit, or during prolonged recovery.

Why DVT Prophylaxis Matters

Deep vein thrombosis is one of the most common — and most preventable — complications following major surgery and periods of immobility. The American College of Chest Physicians and other major clinical bodies recommend mechanical prophylaxis as a first-line or adjunctive intervention for moderate- and high-risk patients, particularly when pharmacologic anticoagulation is contraindicated.

Venous Systems supplies clinically validated intermittent pneumatic compression (IPC) devices that deliver hospital-grade compression in a portable, patient-friendly form factor.

Common Indications

  • Post-operative recovery from orthopedic surgery (TKA, THA, spine, fracture repair)
  • Post-operative recovery from abdominal, pelvic, or oncologic surgery
  • Patients with a prior DVT or pulmonary embolism history
  • Patients on contraindicated anticoagulant therapy
  • Prolonged bed rest, cast immobilization, or limb non-weight bearing
  • High-risk patients undergoing long-distance air travel

How Our Devices Work

Each portable IPC system from Venous Systems uses sequential or uniform pneumatic compression to mimic the calf muscle pump. Sleeves cyclically inflate, pushing venous blood toward the heart and preventing the stasis that drives clot formation. Devices are battery-powered for true mobility — patients can wear them while sleeping, walking around the house, or traveling.

Why portable IPC over hospital-tethered pumps: Patient compliance is the single largest determinant of DVT prophylaxis efficacy. Portable, quiet, battery-powered devices dramatically improve wear time at home compared to bulky tethered units.

Insurance & Documentation

DVT prophylaxis devices are routinely covered under Medicare (E0676), Medicaid in most states, workers' compensation, and commercial plans when prescribed for an appropriate diagnosis. Our team verifies coverage, secures prior authorization, and prepares all documentation in advance of shipping.

Rental and Purchase

For post-surgical patients, rental periods typically span 2–6 weeks. For chronic conditions or recurrent DVT history, purchase may be more cost-effective; our specialists work with the prescriber to recommend the right option for each case.
