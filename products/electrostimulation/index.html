TENS & NMES Electrostimulation

Transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS) and neuromuscular electrical stimulation (NMES) units that modulate pain and re-engage muscle — without medication.

TENS: Drug-Free Pain Modulation

TENS units deliver low-voltage electrical impulses through skin electrodes placed near the source of pain. The current interferes with pain signal transmission at the nerve level and may trigger the body's release of endogenous endorphins. The result is meaningful pain relief without the side effects, addiction risk, or fall risk associated with opioid analgesics.

TENS is one of the most widely prescribed non-pharmacologic pain interventions in the United States, and is recommended by clinical guidelines for a range of acute and chronic pain conditions.

NMES: Muscle Re-Education

NMES uses higher-intensity electrical impulses to cause active muscle contraction. It is used in post-injury and post-surgical rehabilitation to re-establish muscle firing patterns, prevent atrophy during immobilization, and accelerate the return of strength. Many of our units offer both TENS and NMES modalities in a single device.

Common Indications

  • Chronic low back pain
  • Cervical pain and tension headache
  • Knee osteoarthritis
  • Post-surgical pain (orthopedic, abdominal, thoracic)
  • Quadriceps weakness following ACL reconstruction or TKA
  • Shoulder rehabilitation following rotator cuff repair
  • Diabetic peripheral neuropathy pain
  • Fibromyalgia and other chronic pain syndromes
The opioid-free advantage: Patients prescribed TENS as part of a multimodal pain plan often report substantially lower reliance on opioid medication during the acute recovery window — making this one of the most impactful non-pharmacologic interventions available.
