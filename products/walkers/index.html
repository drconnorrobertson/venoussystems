Walkers & Mobility Aids

Getting the patient up and walking is one of the most important interventions in any recovery plan. We supply the right mobility aid for the patient, the home, and the prescribing provider's goals.

Why Mobility Aids Matter for Recovery

Early ambulation is one of the most consistent predictors of recovery success after surgery, fall, or extended hospitalization. The right walker reduces fall risk, restores confidence, and lets the patient build cardiovascular and musculoskeletal endurance without compromising healing tissue. The wrong walker — too tall, too narrow, too unstable — keeps patients sitting and slows recovery.

Venous Systems matches the device to the patient, the diagnosis, and the home environment.

Mobility Categories We Supply

Standard Walkers

Lightweight aluminum walkers — folding, two-wheeled, or rigid — for patients who need maximum stability and are partially weight-bearing. Adjustable height, foam grips, optional glide tips.

Four-Wheel Rollators

Wheeled rollators with hand brakes and integrated seat, designed for community ambulation. Ideal for patients who need rest breaks or who use mobility aids primarily outside the home. Available in standard, heavy-duty bariatric, and lightweight travel models.

Hemi Walkers

Single-handed walkers for patients with one-sided weakness — typically post-stroke or following unilateral upper-extremity injury. Provides far more stability than a quad cane.

Knee Walkers / Scooters

For patients who are non-weight-bearing on one lower extremity following ankle, foot, or distal tibia surgery — knee walkers preserve mobility while protecting the surgical site.

Bariatric Walkers

Reinforced walkers and rollators rated for higher weight capacities, with wider frames for added comfort and stability.

Fit matters: A walker that's too tall causes shoulder strain and unstable gait. A walker that's too short rounds the back and shifts weight forward. Our specialists confirm correct sizing for every patient before shipment.

Common Indications

  • Total hip or knee arthroplasty recovery
  • Fracture recovery (hip, pelvis, lower extremity)
  • Post-stroke rehabilitation
  • Parkinson's disease and other neuromuscular conditions
  • Generalized weakness following hospitalization or illness
  • Fall risk in community-dwelling older adults

What Ships With Every Order

  • The selected walker or rollator, pre-adjusted to the patient's measurements
  • Accessories appropriate to the model (basket, tray, glide tips, etc.)
  • Written instructions on safe gait pattern with the device
  • Fit verification call within one week
  • Patient support line for any questions

Insurance & Documentation

Walkers and rollators are covered under Medicare, Medicaid, workers' compensation, auto/no-fault, TRICARE, and most commercial plans when medically necessary. HCPCS coverage varies by device class. Our billing team identifies the appropriate code, secures prior authorization where required, and prepares the medical necessity documentation.
