Back Braces & Spinal Orthoses

Lumbar-sacral orthoses, thoracolumbar braces, and rigid spinal stabilization — sized to the patient, shipped to the home, fit-verified by phone.

Bracing as Part of Opioid-Free Recovery

For many back conditions, mechanical stabilization is the single most effective non-pharmacologic intervention available. A well-fitted brace unloads injured discs, restricts motion at unstable segments, supports healing fusion sites, and gives the patient confidence to move and return to function. Done correctly, it dramatically reduces the amount of pain medication a patient needs.

Venous Systems carries the full range of spinal orthoses from rigid post-op TLSOs to flexible lumbar supports for chronic conditions, all sized and shipped with fit verification.

Bracing Categories We Supply

Lumbar-Sacral Orthoses (LSO)

Supportive braces covering the lumbar and sacral regions, used for acute lumbar strain, chronic low back pain, spinal stenosis, and post-discectomy recovery. Available in standard sizing and adjustable models with rigid panels for added support.

Thoracolumbar-Sacral Orthoses (TLSO)

Higher-coverage braces extending from the upper back through the sacrum, used after vertebral compression fractures, multi-level fusion, and significant thoracolumbar instability. Available in rigid clamshell and modular formats.

Rigid Spinal Stabilization Braces

Custom-molded or modular rigid braces for the most demanding post-surgical cases, including complex multi-level instrumentation and trauma recovery. We coordinate with the surgical team on fit and wear protocol.

Cervical & Cervicothoracic Braces

Soft collars, rigid cervical orthoses, and cervicothoracic stabilization braces are also available through our catalog on request.

Common Indications

  • Acute lumbar strain or chronic mechanical low back pain
  • Lumbar fusion or discectomy recovery
  • Vertebral compression fracture (osteoporotic or traumatic)
  • Spondylolisthesis and spinal instability
  • Spinal stenosis with conservative management
  • Post-trauma stabilization per surgical protocol
Fit verified, not just shipped: Every brace order ships with a sizing chart and is followed up by a Venous Systems specialist who confirms fit by phone or video. If the brace doesn't fit correctly, we exchange it — no charge to the patient or provider.
